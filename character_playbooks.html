Character playbooks

These are the rules I've been using in various games of D&D for the last ten years or so. I play a lot with new players and with very short sessions, so the rules are optimised for fast play and quick pickup.

There are a million OSR rulesets in the world already, so I don't think this is going to change your life or anything, but there are a few ideas I think are worth stealing:

  • Character sheets as class playbooks.
  • No to-hit roll.
  • Class-specific pick-lists for equipment.
  • Demi-humans which work very differently from humans.
  • Stunt mechanics.
